Engines

Multirotor drones, such as quadcopters, have motors (generally 4, 6, or 8) that generate the thrust needed for flight. There are two main types of motors used in drones:
- Brushless Motors: Offer greater efficiency and durability.
- Brushed Motors: More affordable but less efficient and durable.

Controllers

Controllers serve as the brain of the drone, managing its flight characteristics and navigation. They receive input from the pilot or onboard sensors and adjust the drone's motors accordingly. Advanced controllers offer features such as GPS navigation, altitude hold, and autonomous flight modes.

Cameras

Cameras are essential for capturing aerial footage and providing real-time video feeds for FPV (First Person View) flying. They come in various resolutions and specifications, including HD, 4K, and thermal imaging. The quality of the camera greatly influences the drone's ability to capture stunning imagery and perform tasks such as aerial surveying and cinematography.
